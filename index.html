Malaysia Household Poverty Analysis Dashboard

Exploring poverty trends, economic growth, and regional inequality across Malaysia using historical and spatial socio-economic indicators.

1. Poverty Distribution Across Malaysian States

Malaysia’s eastern states continue facing significantly higher poverty levels than western urban regions. The map highlights Sabah as the country’s most vulnerable state during 2022. Economic development remains uneven despite long-term national poverty reduction efforts.

2. Poverty Reduction and GDP Growth Trends

Malaysia’s poverty rate steadily declined alongside long-term national economic growth. Periods of slower GDP growth briefly affected poverty reduction performance nationwide. The chart demonstrates how economic expansion contributed towards improved living standards.

3. Poverty Composition Changes Over Time

Hardcore poverty reduced substantially throughout recent decades across Malaysia. Absolute poverty remained consistently larger than hardcore poverty measurements nationally. Government development programs contributed towards reducing severe financial hardship levels.

4. State Poverty Rankings

Several Malaysian states maintain relatively low poverty levels compared with national averages. Sabah continues recording significantly higher poverty percentages than most other regions. The ranking chart clearly highlights strong regional inequality throughout the country.

5. Poverty Difference From National Average

States above the national baseline experience greater poverty pressures than average. Negative deviations represent states performing better than the national poverty benchmark. Sabah exceeds the national average by a considerably large margin.

6. Human Development Index by State

Higher HDI scores generally indicate stronger healthcare, education, and living conditions. Kuala Lumpur demonstrates the country’s highest overall human development performance. Lower HDI regions commonly experience greater poverty and economic vulnerability challenges.

7. Historical Poverty Range by State

Historical poverty volatility differs greatly between Malaysian states over time. Wider ranges suggest unstable economic resilience and changing poverty conditions. Sabah experienced the largest long-term poverty fluctuations among all states analysed.

8. Historical Poverty Trends Among Highest States

Most Malaysian states gradually reduced poverty rates through economic development progress. Sabah consistently remained among the highest poverty trajectories historically nationwide. The chart reveals unequal poverty reduction speeds between different Malaysian regions.

9. Absolute and Relative Poverty Comparison

Relative poverty remains visible even within economically developed urban states. Absolute poverty measures severe financial hardship affecting basic household survival needs. The comparison reveals continuing inequality despite broader national economic development.

10. State Comparison Between Poverty Categories

Relative poverty percentages exceed absolute poverty rates across many Malaysian states. Economic growth alone does not completely remove inequality within modern societies. Regional economic structures strongly influence household financial vulnerability patterns.

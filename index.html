Malaysia Household Poverty Analysis Dashboard

An interactive spatial and statistical exploration of historical macroeconomic boundaries and sub-national vulnerability metrics.

1. Choropleth Geospatial Map

Spatial distribution mapping absolute poverty rates across Malaysian states.

2. Dual-Axis Macro Layer

Tracing the historical inverse tracking relationship between GDP per capita and baseline absolute poverty lines.

3. Normalized Streamgraph

Visualizing shifts in proportional distribution weights between hardcore and absolute metrics over time.

4. Absolute Poverty Proportions

Cross-sectional bar chart highlighting the accumulated proportions of absolute poverty metrics by state.

5. Diverging Bar Deviation

Quantifying individual state level performance variances relative to the calculated national benchmark baseline.

6. Sub-national Human Development Index (HDI)

Ranked baseline profiles utilizing UN development metrics to evaluate structural socio-economic parameters across regions.

7. Historical Volatility Range Envelope

Comprehensive profile tracking extreme performance boundaries and historical low/high spreads for each unique state.

8. Faceted Area Small Multiples

Independent multi-panel trend analysis isolating individual trajectory paths against a static reference threshold marker.

9. Interactive Distribution Scatter

Evaluating multi-dimensional coordinates with custom slider filters to dynamically isolate structural parameters.

10. Poverty Type Side-by-Side Comparison

A paired comparative analysis analyzing the structural gap between absolute and relative indicators per region.

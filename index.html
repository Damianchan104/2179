Malaysia Household Poverty Analysis Dashboard

Exploring poverty trends, economic growth, and regional inequalities across Malaysia.

1. Poverty Distribution Across Malaysian States

Sabah records the country's highest absolute poverty rate.

Urban regions generally show lower poverty concentrations.

East Malaysia displays deeper regional inequality patterns.

2. Poverty Reduction and GDP Growth Trends

Poverty steadily declined during Malaysia's economic expansion period.

GDP growth strongly improved after the early 1990s.

Economic downturns briefly slowed poverty reduction progress.

3. Poverty Composition Changes Over Time

Hardcore poverty declined significantly across recent decades.

Absolute poverty remained larger than hardcore poverty nationally.

Development programs reduced extreme financial hardship levels.

4. State Poverty Rankings

Sabah remains the most vulnerable state nationally.

Several states maintain relatively moderate poverty rates.

Wealth distribution differs greatly between Malaysian regions.

5. Poverty Difference From National Average

Positive bars indicate poverty above national average levels.

Negative bars represent states below national poverty averages.

Sabah exceeds the national baseline by large margins.

6. Human Development Index by State

Higher HDI scores indicate stronger living conditions overall.

Kuala Lumpur records Malaysia's highest development levels.

Lower HDI states often experience higher poverty challenges.

7. Historical Poverty Range by State

Wide ranges indicate unstable long-term poverty performance patterns.

Narrow ranges suggest stronger economic stability over time.

Sabah experienced the largest historical poverty fluctuations nationally.

8. Historical Poverty Trends Among Highest States

Sabah maintained Malaysia's highest poverty trajectory historically.

Most states gradually reduced poverty through economic development.

Poverty reduction speed differs between individual Malaysian states.

9. Absolute and Relative Poverty Comparison

Relative poverty remains higher within developed urban regions.

Absolute poverty measures basic household survival difficulties.

Relative poverty reflects inequality within modern living standards.

10. State Comparison Between Poverty Categories

Relative poverty exceeds absolute poverty across many states.

Economic growth does not eliminate inequality completely nationwide.

Poverty experiences vary between regional economic structures.
